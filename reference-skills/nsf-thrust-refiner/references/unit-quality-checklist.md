# Unit Quality Checklist

Use this checklist after refining a thrust or aim.

- The unit title is specific and aligned with the proposal family.
- The opening paragraph states a real bottleneck, not only a broad topic area.
- The gap paragraph explains why prior work is insufficient for this proposal's setting.
- The technical move is concrete enough that a reviewer can imagine what will be built or studied.
- The unit includes at least one compact technical object or formalism when the method would otherwise read as generic prose.
- Each subtask has a distinct role.
- The unit names at least one local measurable outcome, milestone, or success criterion.
- Dependencies on earlier or later units are explicit where helpful.
- Citations support the strongest claims.
- Unresolved citations or collaborator facts are surfaced with visible `\todo{...}` markers.
- If a figure would materially compress the unit logic, the file includes a concrete figure suggestion and placeholder scaffold or TODO.
